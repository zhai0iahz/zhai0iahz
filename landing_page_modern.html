Meet Jason

Jason is an educator, philosopher, and performer bringing a renaissance approach to tennis coaching. Located near Irvine Spectrum Center, he specializes in working with beginners and those from non-athletic backgrounds—academics, gamers, and anyone seeking to discover their unique tennis journey.

Rather than forcing conventional methods, Jason meets students where they are. He believes tennis should be accessible, meaningful, and authentically yours.

"I am an advocate for finding one's own tennis, no matter how unconventional."

His teaching philosophy: Fuel the passion, Burn the waste, Save the lessons. Through gaming mechanics, psychological coaching, and genuine mentorship, Jason helps students move beyond fear and self-doubt to discover their authentic tennis potential.

The Court & Craft Philosophy

We believe great coaching is personalized, inclusive, and transformative. Here's what sets us apart:

Gaming Mechanics

We explain tennis through frameworks you already understand—game theory, progression systems, strategic thinking. Makes learning intuitive and engaging.

Your Unique Style

Tennis isn't one-size-fits-all. We help you discover your natural playing style and develop it with authenticity and confidence.

Psychology-First

Technical skills matter, but mindset is everything. We address fear, doubt, and performance anxiety head-on through proven psychological methods.

Beginner-Friendly

No athletic background? No problem. We specialize in bringing unconventional learners into the game with joy, confidence, and respect.

Mentorship Model

Jason is more than a coach—he's an educator and mentor invested in your growth as a player and person.

Sustainable Growth

Fuel your passion, burn away bad habits, and save the lessons learned. We build for long-term love of the game.
