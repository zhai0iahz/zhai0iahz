Meet Jason

Jason is a renaissance man—educator, philosopher, and performer. Near Irvine Spectrum Center, he's revolutionizing how tennis is taught by meeting students where they are, regardless of their athletic background.

Specializing in private lessons for beginners and those from non-athletic backgrounds, Jason brings a philosophical approach to coaching. He's passionate about helping academics, gamers, and thrill-seekers discover their own authentic tennis journey.

"I am an advocate for finding one's own tennis, no matter how unconventional."

His unique philosophy: Fuel the passion, Burn the waste, Save the lessons. Jason doesn't force a one-size-fits-all approach. Instead, he teaches through gaming mechanics, psychology, and genuine mentorship.

The Court & Craft Approach

Tennis isn't one-size-fits-all. Here's how we break the mold:

Gaming Mechanics

We explain tennis concepts through RPG and gaming frameworks. XP, levels, cooldowns, and strategic thinking—because you already speak that language.

Find Your Style

Your tennis identity isn't predetermined. Whether you're a power player, precision artist, or strategic thinker, we help you discover and develop YOUR game.

Philosophy First

What does tennis mean to YOU? We explore the deeper reasons behind the sport—discipline, creativity, resilience—and weave that into every lesson.

Beginner-Friendly

No athletic background required. We specialize in bringing academics, gamers, and unconventional thinkers into the game with confidence and joy.

Mentorship Model

Jason isn't just a coach—he's a mentor, educator, and performer. Every lesson is a chance to grow as a person, not just a player.

Fuel. Burn. Save.

Fuel the passion for the game. Burn the waste (bad habits, self-doubt). Save the lessons learned for a lifetime of tennis joy.
